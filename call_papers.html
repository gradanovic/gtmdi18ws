Call for Papers

All submitted contributions will be peer-reviewed and evaluated on the basis of the quality of submission, originality, and breath of interest. We especially welcome contributions on emerging topics that have the potential to encourage discussions. Industrial applications and position papers that present novel ideas, issues, challenges and directions are also welcome. Specifically we solicit the following two types of submissions:

  1. Research papers that report the results of new, recent, or ongoing research, emphasizing either on theory side or application side or both.
  2. Challenge/Position papers describe a vision for how data elicitation for AI systems should evolve as a field.

To foster and encourage a wide participation and discussion, there will be no formal workshop proceedings. All accepted papers (posters, abstract, preliminary draft as authors prefer) will be posted on our website to the benefit of the participants to the workshop. Submissions of preliminary work and papers to be submitted or in preparation for submission to other major venues in the field are encouraged. Topics of Interest

Submissions are invited in, but not limited to, the following topics:

  • Quality control of crowdsourced data
  • Economic perspective of information elicitation
  • Data market and reputation system for data elicitation
  • Incentives for high quality data from strategic agents
  • Knowledge-free methods for eliciting high quality information
  • Information elicitation with human behavioral models
  • Reinforcement learning with human inputs
  • Safety in machine learning with human inputs
  • Machine learning with human-in-the-loop
  • Data collection for specific applications (healthcare, law enforcement, urban planning, etc.)
